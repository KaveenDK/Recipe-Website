Coconut Cake (Bibikkan)

Home


★★★★★ 278 Ratings

Description:

Bibikkan is a traditional Sri Lankan coconut cake that is rich, moist, and packed with flavor. Made with grated coconut, jaggery (or sugar), rice flour, and a blend of spices, this cake is a beloved treat during festive occasions and special celebrations. Bibikkan has a dense, fudgy texture and a delightful combination of sweetness and spice, making it a favorite among both locals and visitors. Its unique flavor profile and cultural significance make it a must-try dessert in Sri Lankan cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups grated coconut (fresh or desiccated)
  • 1 cup jaggery, grated (or brown sugar)
  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg powder
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup cashews or almonds, chopped (optional)
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

  1. If using jaggery, grate it finely. If using brown sugar, ensure it is free of lumps.
  2. If using desiccated coconut, soak it in warm water for 10 minutes, then drain.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups grated coconut, 1 cup rice flour, 1/2 tsp cardamom powder, 1/4 tsp nutmeg powder, 1/4 tsp cinnamon powder, and 1/4 tsp salt. Mix well.

Step 3: Add the Wet Ingredients

  1. In a separate bowl, whisk 2 eggs until frothy. Add 1/4 cup thick coconut milk and mix well.
  2. Gradually add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring until everything is well combined.

Step 4: Add Optional Ingredients

  1. Fold in 1/4 cup chopped cashews or almonds and 1/4 cup raisins (if using).

Step 5: Bake the Cake

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. Grease a baking pan and line it with parchment paper.
  3. Pour the batter into the pan and spread it evenly.
  4. Bake for 30-40 minutes, or until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

  1. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.
  2. Slice and serve as a dessert or snack.

Tips for Perfect Bibikkan:

  • Use freshly grated coconut for the best flavor and texture. If using desiccated coconut, soak it in warm water to rehydrate.
  • Adjust the sweetness by adding more or less jaggery or sugar.
  • For a richer flavor, toast the spices (cardamom, nutmeg, and cinnamon) before adding them to the batter.
  • Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.
  • Serve with a cup of tea or coffee for a delightful treat.

Cultural Significance:

Bibikkan is a cherished dessert in Sri Lankan cuisine, often prepared during festive occasions such as Sinhala and Tamil New Year, weddings, and religious celebrations. Its rich, dense texture and aromatic spices reflect the island’s love for coconut and traditional flavors. Bibikkan is not just a cake but a symbol of celebration and togetherness, often shared with family and friends. Its preparation and enjoyment are deeply rooted in Sri Lankan culture, making it a timeless and beloved treat.
