Pittu

Description:

Pittu is a traditional Sri Lankan dish made from a combination of rice flour and grated coconut, steamed to create a light, crumbly texture. This simple yet flavorful dish is a staple in Sri Lankan households, often served for breakfast or dinner. Pittu is typically paired with coconut milk, curries, or sambols, making it a versatile and satisfying meal. Its unique texture and subtle coconut flavor make it a favorite among both locals and visitors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 cup grated coconut (fresh or desiccated)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water (as needed, approximately 1 cup)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pittu Mixture

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of rice flour, 1 cup of grated coconut, and 1/2 tsp salt.
  2. Gradually add water, a little at a time, while mixing with your hands. The mixture should resemble coarse crumbs and hold together when pressed lightly.

Step 2: Assemble the Pittu Maker

  1. Fill the bottom section of the pittu maker or steamer with water and bring it to a boil.
  2. Layer the pittu mixture into the pittu maker, alternating with thin layers of grated coconut (optional for extra flavor).

Step 3: Steam the Pittu

  1. Place the pittu maker on the steamer and steam for 8-10 minutes, or until the pittu is cooked through and firm.
  2. To check if it’s done, insert a skewer into the pittu. If it comes out clean, the pittu is ready.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Carefully remove the pittu from the steamer and let it cool slightly.
  2. Serve warm with coconut milk, curry, or sambol.

Tips for Perfect Pittu:

  • Use fine rice flour for a smoother texture. If the flour is coarse, sieve it before use.
  • The mixture should be crumbly but moist enough to hold its shape when pressed. Adjust the water quantity as needed.
  • For extra flavor, add a pinch of cumin seeds or cardamom powder to the mixture.
  • If you don’t have a pittu maker, you can use a steamer or a colander lined with cheesecloth.
  • Serve pittu immediately after steaming for the best texture and flavor.

Cultural Significance:

Pittu is a beloved dish in Sri Lankan cuisine, often enjoyed as a comforting breakfast or dinner. It is a symbol of simplicity and resourcefulness, showcasing the use of readily available ingredients like rice flour and coconut. In rural areas, pittu is traditionally made fresh each morning, reflecting the close connection between Sri Lankan culture and its agricultural roots. Paired with creamy coconut milk or spicy curries, pittu represents the harmonious blend of flavors and textures that define Sri Lankan cooking.
