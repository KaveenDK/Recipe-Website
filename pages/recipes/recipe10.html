Wood Apple Juice (Divul Juice)

Home


★★★★★ 278 Ratings

Description:

Wood Apple Juice, known as Divul Juice in Sri Lanka, is a refreshing and nutritious drink made from the pulp of the wood apple fruit. The wood apple, or divul, has a unique flavor sweet, tangy, and slightly sour making it a popular and healthy beverage. This juice is not only delicious but also packed with vitamins, minerals, and natural digestive benefits.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe wood apple (divul)
  • 2-3 cups water (adjust for desired consistency)
  • 2-3 tbsp sugar (adjust to taste)
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • Ice cubes (for serving)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Wood Apple

  1. Crack open the wood apple using a knife or by tapping it gently on a hard surface.
  2. Scoop out the pulp using a spoon and remove any seeds or hard parts.

Step 2: Blend the Pulp

  1. Place the wood apple pulp in a blender.
  2. Add 1 cup of water and blend until smooth.

Step 3: Strain the Mixture

  1. Strain the blended mixture through a sieve or muslin cloth to remove any fibrous bits.
  2. Use a spoon to press the pulp and extract as much juice as possible.

Step 4: Sweeten and Flavor

  1. Add sugar and a pinch of salt to the strained juice. Mix well until the sugar dissolves.
  2. For a spicy twist, add chili powder (optional).

Step 5: Adjust Consistency

  1. Add the remaining 1-2 cups of water (or more) to achieve your desired consistency.
  2. Stir well and taste. Adjust the sweetness or saltiness if needed.

Step 6: Serve

  1. Pour the juice into glasses over ice cubes.
  2. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Tips for Perfect Wood Apple Juice:

  • Use a ripe wood apple for the best flavor. The fruit should have a strong, sweet aroma.
  • Adjust the amount of sugar and water to suit your taste preferences.
  • For a creamier texture, you can add a splash of coconut milk.
  • If you prefer a smoother juice, strain it twice to remove all fibrous bits.

Cultural Significance:

Wood Apple Juice is a beloved beverage in Sri Lanka, often enjoyed during hot weather for its refreshing and hydrating properties. The wood apple fruit is also considered a natural remedy for digestive issues and is rich in antioxidants. This drink is a testament to Sri Lanka’s tropical abundance and its tradition of using natural ingredients to create flavorful and healthy beverages.
